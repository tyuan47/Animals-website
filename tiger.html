The Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger is currently an endangered species. They mostly live in India and other parts of Asia as the apex predator. They help maintain the enviorment they are in and it is said that with one bengal tiger 25,000 acres of forest can be protected. Trading and poaching of the tigers has only recently been banned. People prize these tigers as status symbols and they are even used in folk cures. They are also suffering because of lack of prey due to human poaching.

Donate here to the organization WWF also known as the World Wildlife Fund. They are helping the Bengal Tiger by securing large areas of habitat throughout the world helping wildlife have a home and reforestation. They also help stop the trade and traffic of wildlife through supporting antipoaching patrols and supporting intelligence networks.
